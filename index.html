Federated ADMM-LASSO

Suppose there are $m$ clients, each with dataset $D_i=\{X_i,y_i\}$ for $i=1,\ldots,m$, where $X_i\in\mathbb{R}^{n_i\times p}$ is the feature matrix with $n_i$ samples and $p$ common predictors, and $y_i\in\mathbb{R}^{n_i}$ is the response vector. Our goal is to estimate a single sparse coefficient vector $\beta\in\mathbb{R}^{p}$ that captures the relationship between predictors and response across all clients while preserving privacy, with no raw-data sharing. We employ the LASSO with squared loss to encourage sparsity. The classical LASSO estimator introduces an $\ell_1$ penalty that shrinks many coefficients exactly to zero, thereby performing simultaneous estimation and variable selection. For client $i$, the local LASSO problem is

$$ \min_{\beta\in\mathbb{R}^{p}} \frac{1}{2}\lVert y_i-X_i\beta\rVert_2^2+\lambda\lVert\beta\rVert_1, $$

where $\lambda>0$ is a regularization parameter that balances model fit and sparsity. We seek a common sparse coefficient vector across centers. To enable distributed optimization, we introduce local copies $\beta_i\in\mathbb{R}^{p}$ and a global consensus parameter $z\in\mathbb{R}^{p}$, and enforce sparsity through the shared parameter $z$. This leads to the following consensus formulation of the LASSO:

$$ \begin{aligned} \min_{\{\beta_i\},z}\quad & \sum_{i=1}^{m}\frac{1}{2}\lVert y_i-X_i\beta_i\rVert_2^2 +\lambda\lVert z\rVert_1 \\ \text{s.t.}\quad & \beta_i=z,\qquad i=1,\ldots,m. \end{aligned} \tag{2} $$

In the consensus formulation, sparsity is enforced through the shared parameter $z$, ensuring that all sites converge to a common sparse coefficient vector. This consensus formulation has been widely used in distributed learning and can be efficiently solved using ADMM. To optimize (2), the scaled dual variables $u_i=\pi_i/\rho\in\mathbb{R}^{p}$ are introduced, where $\pi_i$ denotes the unscaled dual variable associated with the consensus constraint $\beta_i=z$ at client $i$, and $\rho>0$ is the ADMM penalty parameter. Following the scaled ADMM formulation, we work directly with the scaled dual variables $u_i$, which simplifies the update equations. Under this formulation, the scaled augmented Lagrangian corresponding to (2) is

$$ \mathcal{L}_{\rho}(\{\beta_i\},z,\{u_i\})= \sum_{i=1}^{m}\left[ \frac{1}{2}\lVert y_i-X_i\beta_i\rVert_2^2+ \frac{\rho}{2}\lVert\beta_i-z+u_i\rVert_2^2 \right]+\lambda\lVert z\rVert_1. \tag{3} $$

Local coefficient update

To derive the closed-form local update for $\beta_i$, differentiate (3) with respect to $\beta_i$, holding $z$ and $u_i$ fixed, and set the result equal to zero:

$$ \frac{\partial}{\partial\beta_i} \mathcal{L}_{\rho}(\{\beta_i\},z,\{u_i\})=0. $$

This gives

$$ \left(X_i^{\top}X_i+\rho I\right)\beta_i =X_i^{\top}y_i+\rho(z-u_i). $$

Therefore, at iteration $k$, client $i$ computes

$$ \beta_i^{k+1}= \left(X_i^{\top}X_i+\rho I\right)^{-1} \left[X_i^{\top}y_i+\rho\left(z^k-u_i^k\right)\right]. $$

Global consensus update

Once all clients have computed the local parameters $\beta_i^{k+1}$, the algorithm updates the global consensus variable $z$. In the ADMM framework, this is done by minimizing (3) with respect to $z$, treating $\beta_i^{k+1}$ and $u_i^k$ as fixed. The $z$ update solves

$$ z^{k+1}=\arg\min_z\left\{ \lambda\lVert z\rVert_1+ \frac{\rho}{2}\sum_{i=1}^{m} \lVert\beta_i^{k+1}-z+u_i^k\rVert_2^2 \right\}. \tag{4} $$

For notational convenience, define $b_i=\beta_i^{k+1}+u_i^k$ and $\bar b=\frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}b_i$. Then problem (4) becomes

$$ \min_z\left\{ \lambda\lVert z\rVert_1+ \frac{\rho}{2}\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert z-b_i\rVert_2^2 \right\}. \tag{5} $$

Using the standard identity, expand the quadratic term in (5):

$$ \begin{aligned} \sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert z-b_i\rVert_2^2 &=\sum_{i=1}^{m}\left(z^{\top}z-2b_i^{\top}z+b_i^{\top}b_i\right)\\ &=m z^{\top}z-2m\bar b^{\top}z+ \sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert b_i\rVert_2^2\\ &=m\left(z^{\top}z-2\bar b^{\top}z+\lVert\bar b\rVert_2^2\right) +\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert b_i\rVert_2^2-m\lVert\bar b\rVert_2^2\\ &=m\lVert z-\bar b\rVert_2^2+ \left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert b_i\rVert_2^2-m\lVert\bar b\rVert_2^2\right). \end{aligned} $$

The term $\left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert b_i\rVert_2^2-m\lVert\bar b\rVert_2^2\right)$ does not depend on $z$ and can therefore be dropped from the objective. Equation (5) is equivalent to solving

$$ \min_z\left\{ \lambda\lVert z\rVert_1+ \frac{m\rho}{2}\lVert z-\bar b\rVert_2^2 \right\}. \tag{6} $$

Problem (6) is separable across coordinates and has the standard form of an $\ell_1$-penalized quadratic problem, whose minimizer is obtained by componentwise soft-thresholding of $\bar b$. In particular, for each coordinate $j=1,\ldots,p$,

$$ z_j^{k+1}= \begin{cases} \operatorname{sign}(\bar b_j) \left(|\bar b_j|-\dfrac{\lambda}{m\rho}\right), & |\bar b_j|>\dfrac{\lambda}{m\rho},\\[8pt] 0, & |\bar b_j|\leq\dfrac{\lambda}{m\rho}. \end{cases} $$

Equivalently, the update can be written compactly using the soft-thresholding operator with scalar argument $t\in\mathbb{R}$ and threshold parameter $\kappa>0$. In this setting, $\kappa=\lambda/(m\rho)$:

$$ S_{\kappa}(t)=\operatorname{sign}(t)\max(|t|-\kappa,0) = \begin{cases} t-\kappa, & t>\kappa,\\ 0, & |t|\leq\kappa,\\ t+\kappa, & t<-\kappa. \end{cases} $$

Applying this operator elementwise to vectors, the global aggregation step is

$$ z^{k+1}=S_{\lambda/(m\rho)}(\bar b) =S_{\lambda/(m\rho)}\left[ \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m} \left(\beta_i^{k+1}+u_i^k\right) \right]. $$

Scaled dual-variable update

After updating the global parameter $z^{k+1}$, the scaled dual variables are updated locally at each site according to

$$ u_i^{k+1}=u_i^k+\beta_i^{k+1}-z^{k+1}, \qquad i=1,\ldots,m. $$

This step accumulates the current consensus mismatch $\beta_i^{k+1}-z^{k+1}$ into the dual variables. When the local parameters and the global parameter are in agreement, the dual updates become negligible and the algorithm approaches a fixed point. The sequence $\{(\beta_i^k,z^k,u_i^k)\}$ is generated by repeatedly applying the local $\beta_i$-update, the global $z$-update, and the dual update. In practice, primal and dual residuals are monitored to determine when to terminate the iterations.

Convergence Diagnostics

The ADMM iterations are terminated when both primal and dual residuals satisfy pre-specified convergence criteria. We use the scaled ADMM form with penalty parameter $\rho>0$. The primal residual measures the consensus mismatch across sites and is defined as

$$ \lVert r^{k+1}\rVert_2= \left( \sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert\beta_i^{k+1}-z^{k+1}\rVert_2^2 \right)^{1/2}. $$

The dual residual measures the change in the consensus variable and is defined as

$$ \lVert s^{k+1}\rVert_2= \rho\sqrt{m}\,\lVert z^{k+1}-z^k\rVert_2. $$

Following standard practice, absolute and relative tolerances $\epsilon_{\mathrm{abs}},\epsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}>0$ are used to construct the stopping thresholds:

$$ \epsilon_{\mathrm{pri}}= \sqrt{mp}\,\epsilon_{\mathrm{abs}}+ \epsilon_{\mathrm{rel}} \max\left\{ \left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert\beta_i^{k+1}\rVert_2^2\right)^{1/2}, \sqrt{m}\,\lVert z^{k+1}\rVert_2 \right\}, $$ $$ \epsilon_{\mathrm{dual}}= \sqrt{mp}\,\epsilon_{\mathrm{abs}}+ \epsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}\rho \left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert u_i^k\rVert_2^2\right)^{1/2}. $$
Stopping rule. The algorithm is terminated when both $\lVert r^{k+1}\rVert_2\leq\epsilon_{\mathrm{pri}}$ and $\lVert s^{k+1}\rVert_2\leq\epsilon_{\mathrm{dual}}$. For this study, $\epsilon_{\mathrm{abs}}=10^{-4}$ and $\epsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}=10^{-3}$, with a maximum number of iterations imposed as a safeguard.

Algorithm: Federated ADMM-LASSO

The complete federated ADMM-LASSO procedure combines the local coefficient update, the global consensus $z$-update, and the local dual update. The whole procedure is summarized in the following algorithm.

Require: local data $\{(X_i,y_i)\}_{i=1}^{m}$; $m$ clients; $\lambda>0$; $\rho>0$; maximum ADMM iterations $K$; absolute tolerance $\epsilon_{\mathrm{abs}}$; relative tolerance $\epsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}$.

Initialize $z^0$ and $u_i^0=0$ for $i=1,\ldots,m$.

for $k=0,1,\ldots,K-1$ do

for all clients $i=1,\ldots,m$ in parallel do

Local update: Client $i$ uses only its local data $(X_i,y_i)$ to compute

$$ \beta_i^{k+1} =\left(X_i^{\top}X_i+\rho I\right)^{-1} \left[X_i^{\top}y_i+\rho\left(z^k-u_i^k\right)\right]. $$

The local data $X_i$ and $y_i$ remain at client $i$; only model-level quantities are used for consensus aggregation.

end for

Global update ($z$-step): Aggregate the local updates and apply componentwise soft-thresholding:

$$ z^{k+1}=S_{\lambda/(m\rho)}\!\left[ \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}\left(\beta_i^{k+1}+u_i^k\right) \right]. $$

for all clients $i=1,\ldots,m$ in parallel do

Dual update:

$$ u_i^{k+1}=u_i^k+\beta_i^{k+1}-z^{k+1}. $$

end for

Compute the primal and dual residuals:

$$ \lVert r^{k+1}\rVert_2= \left(\sum_{i=1}^{m}\lVert\beta_i^{k+1}-z^{k+1}\rVert_2^2\right)^{1/2}, \qquad \lVert s^{k+1}\rVert_2= \rho\sqrt{m}\,\lVert z^{k+1}-z^k\rVert_2. $$

Stopping criteria: Stop if $\lVert r^{k+1}\rVert_2\leq\epsilon_{\mathrm{pri}}$ and $\lVert s^{k+1}\rVert_2\leq\epsilon_{\mathrm{dual}}$.

end for

return $z^{\mathrm{final}}$ (final global sparse coefficient vector).
